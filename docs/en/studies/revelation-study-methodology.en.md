## Purpose

This document turns the 7-stage method from the in-app `Preface` article into a reusable working standard for the `Rev. 1:1-3` article and for later Revelation articles.

It complements, rather than replaces, the existing local working file:

- [Rev. 1:1-3 Stage 1 Working Dossier (currently RU only)](../../ru/studies/revelation-1-1-3-research.ru.md)

## The Seven Stages

1. Forming the shared text from all primary witnesses.
2. Dictionary and sentence structure.
3. Identifying and analyzing Old Testament allusions.
4. The Revelation of Jesus Christ.
5. Structural and contextual links.
6. Fulfillment of prophecy.
7. Living faith in action.

Practical logic:

- stages `1-2` secure accurate reading;
- stages `3-5` secure meaning, context, and depth;
- stages `6-7` secure historical testing, application, and spiritual response.

## Source Hierarchy

For stages `3-7`, source priority should remain stable:

| Priority | Source | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `1` | Classical translations | Multilingual comparison layer |
| `2` | Jon Paulien | Primary methodological and interpretive guide |
| `3` | SDA commentaries | Confessionally aligned commentary |
| `4` | Official SDA doctrines | Doctrinal boundaries and official church language |
| `5` | Ellen G. White | Prophetic and spiritual dimension |
| `6` | Other SDA authors and official lessons | Broader internal verification |
| `7` | Early Reformers | Interpretation history and early Protestant layer |

Additional rules:

- stage `1` is governed by manuscripts, transcriptions, collations, and textual critics;
- stage `2` is governed by lexica, grammars, morphology, and syntax tools;
- stage `6` must be externally checked through church historians and secular historians.

## Evidence Rules

- Every direct quotation in the final article must include a concrete link.
- For Jon Paulien, Ellen White, and the `SDA Bible Commentary`, cite chapter, page, paragraph, or verse block whenever possible.
- Every substantial paragraph should have its own evidence map: `claim`, `source`, `type of conclusion`, `confidence`.
- No theological synthesis before stages `1-2` are complete.
- No fulfillment argument before stages `3-5` are complete.
- Disputed historical claims in stage `6` need double control:
  - one primary or near-primary historical witness;
  - one academic control source.
- Weak mirrors and questionable PDF repositories do not belong in the required core set.

## Assembly Template for Every Article

Each passage should be built through the same package:

1. `Text Dossier`
2. `Language Dossier`
3. `OT Matrix`
4. `Christ Matrix`
5. `Structure Map`
6. `History Matrix`
7. `Faith/Application Sheet`

Then the article itself is layered as:

1. short introduction;
2. main exposition from stages `1-5`;
3. historical-prophetic block from stage `6`;
4. pastoral-spiritual block from stage `7`;
5. visual, audio, and video inserts.

## First Pass for Rev. 1:1-3

Next working order:

1. Expand the already existing stage `1` through `CNTR`, `NTVMR`, `CSNTM`, `Codex Sinaiticus`, and `ECM`.
2. Build stage `2` around `αποκαλυψις`, `εδωκεν`, `εσημανεν`, `μαρτυρια`, `τηρουντες`, `καιρος`, `εγγυς`.
3. In stage `3`, test `Dan. 2`, `Dan. 12`, and covenant-obedience formulas first.
4. In stage `4`, show the revelation chain: `God -> Christ -> angel -> John -> church`.
5. In stage `5`, show how `Rev. 1:1-3` programs the whole prologue.
6. In stage `6`, collect the interpretation history of `soon` and `the time is near`.
7. In stage `7`, return the passage to the axis `read -> hear -> keep`.

Minimum visual package:

- manuscript fragments from `01`, `02`, `04`, `046`, `025`;
- textual-variant table;
- revelation-chain diagram;
- key Greek words table;
- OT allusion map;
- prologue structure chart;
- interpretation timeline for `soon / near`.

## Final Orientation

- First the text.
- Then the language.
- Then the Old Testament background.
- Then Christ.
- Then structure.
- Then historical fulfillment.
- Then living faith.

At every stage, Jon Paulien should remain a primary guide.
